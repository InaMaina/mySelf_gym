Dumbbell Thrusters: Stand with your heels shoulder width apart, turning your toes out slightly. Hold the weights up at shoulder level, making sure your elbows are pushing together. Sit back into a deep squat. From there, drive straight up through the heels, pressing your arms overhead. 12 reps

Dumbbell Swing: Holding one dumbbell in your right hand, stand with your feet a little wider than shoulder width apart. Hold your right arm in line with your hip crease. From there, hinge back, holding the dumbbell between your legs. Swing your right arm up to shoulder height. 12 reps per side

  • Most foods and drinks contain calories. Some foods, such as lettuce, contain few calories (1 cup of shredded lettuce has less than 10 calories). Other foods, like peanuts, contain a lot of calories (½ cup of peanuts has 427 calories).You can find out how many calories are in a food by looking at the nutrition facts label. The label also will describe the components of the food — how many grams of carbohydrate, protein, and fat it contains.Here's how many calories are in 1 gram of each:
  • carbohydrate — 4 calories
  • protein — 4 calories
  • fat — 9 calories
  • That means if you know how many grams of each one are in a food, you can calculate the total calories. You would multiply the number of grams by the number of calories in a gram of that food component. For example, if a serving of potato chips (about 20 chips) has 10 grams of fat, 90 calories are from fat. That's 10 grams x 9 calories per gram.
